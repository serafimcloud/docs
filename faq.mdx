<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Do I need to pay to use 21st?">
    Browsing and searching the Marketplace is always free. On the free
    free tier you get **2 free component code retrievals or installs per
    day** across the web, CLI, and MCP. Search is free everywhere. **21st AI generation**
    (Sketch and Code) is a paid feature — subscribe to a paid plan
    ([Builder or Team](/ai/pricing)) with AI enabled to generate, which also
    removes the daily marketplace limits.
  </Accordion>

<Accordion title="What do the paid plans include?">
  Individuals use **Builder**, which has AI off and no monthly credits by
  default. Turning AI on immediately adds a choice of **500, 1,000, or 2,000
  credits/month**, private personal components, and priority generations.
  **Team** seats also start with AI off. AI-enabled seats can use 500, 1,000, or
  2,000 credits per seat each month, combined in one shared pool, with team
  features and centralized billing.
  Every paid plan also unlocks unlimited Marketplace code retrievals, copies,
  and installs. Search stays free on every plan. Need more credits? Buy a pack
  of **+100 for $5**; pack credits roll over. See [Pricing &
  Credits](/ai/pricing).
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How do I cancel my subscription?">
  Go to [Settings → Billing](https://21st.dev/settings/billing) and click
  **Cancel plan**. Cancelling takes effect at the end of your current billing
  period — you keep access until then, no need to contact support.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="I accidentally chose yearly billing instead of quarterly. What can I do?">
  Contact support promptly. An individual **Builder** yearly purchase can be
  reviewed for a switch to quarterly and a refund of the difference. Builder
  with AI enabled, **Team**, legacy prices, taxed invoices, and invoices with
  unusual credits or adjustments need manual review and are not covered by that
  base Builder exception.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How do I get an invoice or receipt?">
  Go to [Settings → Billing](https://21st.dev/settings/billing) and click
  **Manage billing** to open the Stripe billing portal. Past invoices and
  downloadable PDF receipts are available there.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How do I use a component I copied from the Marketplace?">
  Open the component page, copy its code, then paste the files into your
  project's components directory, install any listed dependencies, and import
  it. Components are copied into your own codebase, not installed as a package —
  see [Components](/community/components) for details.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="I bought a template — who do I contact for support or a refund?">
  Templates listed on 21st.dev are sold directly by third-party sellers, not by
  21st. Contact the seller for support, delivery issues, or refunds. If a
  listing looks misleading or broken, also let us know at support@21st.dev so we
  can review it.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Why isn't my Published component in the public catalog?">
  Publishing and catalog featuring are separate. A **Published** component is
  available by direct link and on your profile. To list it in the catalog, open
  its actions on the Studio **Components** page and choose **Submit for
  featuring**. The status changes to **In review**, then to **Featured** or
  **Needs changes**. See the [Quality
  Guidelines](/publishing/quality-guidelines) for details.
</Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I got an email demanding payment for a 'security vulnerability' — is that real?">
    No. 21st does not run a paid bug bounty program, and we never pay under
    pressure. Ignore emails that threaten to publish or exploit a
    "vulnerability" unless paid. If you've found a genuine security issue,
    report it responsibly to support@21st.dev.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
